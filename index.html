About Me

Hi, I'm Yina!

I am a MS student in Computer Science at Tennessee State University, Nashville.

Real-world electronic health record (EHR) data offers valuable insights into patient health and disease patterns, but its complexity makes it difficult to analyze directly. My research focuses on using machine learning and causal models to better understand how different features in EHR data, like lab test results or vital signs, impact patient outcomes. By identifying which factors are most important and understanding their cause-and-effect relationships, I aim to uncover key insights that can help improve patient care. This work can lead to better treatments, more personalized healthcare, and smarter use of medical resources.

I work with Dr. Manar Samad on the real-world EHR data. Currently, I am working on investigating feature importance in EHR data through causal discovery algorithms.
